# The Backwater Mining Collective
## Backwater Miners: Appalachian Pioneers of the Digital Frontier

### The Overmountain Men

History remembers the Overmountain Men as rugged pioneers who defied a king, crossed the Appalachian Mountains, and built the first independent, self-governing communities in America, like the Watauga Association. They were the farmers, hunters, and explorers who sought a life of self-sovereignty on the new frontier.

But to the centralized British power and the coastal elites they left behind, they were not heroes. They were "backwater men."

It was an insult, a way to dismiss them as uncultured, "backward," and insignificant. They were underestimated. But when their autonomy was threatened, these "backwater men" mustered, marched, and decisively turned the tide of the American Revolution at King's Mountain.

Today, the revolution we fight for is one of a decentralized, digital future. And once again, the small, independent operators are dismissed as insignificant by a new set of centralized powers.

The spirit of these men inspired the Backwater Mining Collective movement. As Backwater Miners, we embrace this name and the resilient, revolutionary character of those who came before us.
